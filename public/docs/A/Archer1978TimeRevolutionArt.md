# The author’s main move (1 sentence)
They try to justify a “revolution” in art/design education by defining **Design** as a third mode of knowing (with its own epistemology and languages) in order to demand institutional resources and scholarly legitimacy. ``[@Archer1978TimeRevolutionArt, p. 4]``

## Claim 2
- **Claim (plain):** The third domain has its own ways of knowing and its own “language”: Design knowledge is practical (sensibility, invention, validation, implementation) and its essential language is modelling.  
- **Evidence (quote/paraphrase + page):** Archer contrasts Science (observation/measurement/theory/test) and Humanities (criticism/evaluation/discourse) with a third body of practical knowledge; he then defines “design awareness” and says the essential language of Design is “modelling”, i.e., representations across media (drawings, diagrams, physical models, gestures, algorithms). ``[@Archer1978TimeRevolutionArt, p. 5–6]``  
- **Warrant (my words):** This is a direct epistemic claim: Design is not “applied science” or “humanities commentary” but a distinct representational-practical mode with its own validity conditions.

# Definitions / terms this changes (only the ones that matter)
- **Third area / Design (big D):** a domain of human experience/skill/understanding tied to configuring/adapting man-made phenomena, distinct from Science and Humanities. ``[@Archer1978TimeRevolutionArt, p. 6]``  
- **Design awareness:** literacy/numeracy-equivalent for doing/making—ability to understand and handle ideas expressed through making. ``[@Archer1978TimeRevolutionArt, p. 6]``  
- **Modelling (as language of Design):** representational media used to capture/explore/transmit design ideas (diagrams, drawings, physical models, gestures, algorithms). ``[@Archer1978TimeRevolutionArt, p. 6]``  
- **Design epistemology:** study of the nature/validity of ways of knowing/believing/feeling in Design. ``[@Archer1978TimeRevolutionArt, p. 8]``
